How this page works

This is your Command Center for the next 8 weeks. Think of it as a parallel track to the new hire's guide — they're focused on learning, you're focused on scaffolding.

  • Checkboxes are your action items. Some have deadlines, most are weekly rhythms.
  • Page links point to reference material you might need.
  • The weekly sections tell you what to focus on. Not everything will apply every week — use your judgment.
  • Sofia Bergmann is a placeholder. The system will replace it with the actual hire's name.

Your role during onboarding

Your new hire's onboarding quality depends mostly on you. Here's what that means concretely:

ResponsibilityWhat it looks like in practice
Set contextExplain team priorities, current projects, where the new hire fits. Don't assume they'll figure it out.
Weekly 1:1sNot status updates — check understanding, energy, confusion. Ask 'what's confusing?' more than 'what did you do?'
Define a Tiny Win (TM only)A small, scoped, achievable task for the first 2 weeks. Gives them a sense of accomplishment early.
Give feedback earlyDon't wait for week 8. Honest, specific feedback at week 2-3 prevents bigger problems later.
Coordinate with BuddyBrief them before Day 1, check in with them at midpoint. Don't duplicate their role.
Escalate if neededIf something feels off — engagement, confusion, fit concerns — loop in People Ops early. A 5-minute heads-up in week 2 prevents a difficult conversation in week 8.

Signals to watch for

Onboarding remotely means you can't rely on hallway observations. Here's what to pay attention to:

Green flags

  • Asking questions in public channels (not just DMs)
  • Shipping early, even if it's small
  • Giving feedback upward — pushing back on things that don't make sense
  • Building relationships beyond their immediate circle
  • Tracking their time consistently

Yellow flags

  • Going quiet — fewer messages, missed check-ins
  • Only talking to their Buddy, never reaching out to others
  • Over-apologizing or hesitating to share opinions
  • Not tracking time or engaging with Notion
  • Saying everything is 'fine' without specifics

If you see yellow flags, name them early. A direct, caring conversation in week 2 is always better than a formal one in week 8.
