Buddy Guide - Sofia Bergmann's Onboarding

Team Member · Buddy Guide ·

🤝 You've been paired with a new Toggler as their Buddy. This isn't a big time commitment — think 20-30 minutes a few times a week for 4 weeks. But it matters more than you'd think. You're the person they can ask the 'obvious' questions to without worrying about looking clueless.

What being a Buddy actually means

Let’s be clear about what this is and isn’t:

You AREYou're NOT
A friendly face and cultural translatorTheir manager or trainer
The person they ask ‘dumb’ questions toResponsible for their performance
A shortcut to unwritten rulesExpected to teach them their job
Someone who proactively reaches outWaiting for them to come to you
A flag-raiser if something feels offThe one who fixes problems

The best buddies are the ones who remember what it was like to be new here. Channel that.

Your Buddy rhythm

Here’s what the time commitment actually looks like:

WhenWhatTime
Before Day 1Send a short Slack DM introducing yourself5 min
Week 12-3 short check-ins (async or sync)~30 min total
Week 21-2 check-ins, answer accumulated questions~20 min total
Week 3Midpoint — one check-in, flag anything to Lead~15 min
Week 4Final check-in, wrap up~15 min

After Week 4, your Buddy duties end. You’ll get a sign-off message from the system. Of course, you can stay in touch — most people do.

Week 1 — Welcome (Apr 20 – Apr 24)

Make a great first impression. This is when the new hire is most overwhelmed and most grateful for a friendly face.

Conversation starters for Week 1

  • What are you most curious about so far?
  • Have you figured out which Slack channels matter for our team?
  • Morning person or night owl? (Helps with async expectations)
  • Any questions that feel too basic to ask your Lead?

Week 2 (Apr 27 – May 1)

They’re past the initial shock. Now they’re starting to form opinions and have real questions.

Conversation starters for Week 2

  • What’s been the most confusing thing so far?
  • Have you had your first coffee chat? How was it?
  • Anything about how we communicate that feels weird or different?

Week 3 — Midpoint (May 4 – May 8)

Checking the temperature. You’re halfway through your Buddy commitment.

If something concerns you — they seem disengaged, confused about expectations, or struggling with something — tell their Lead privately. That’s not gossiping, that’s being a good Buddy.

Week 4 — Wrap-up (May 11 – May 15)

Your last week as their official Buddy. Make it count.

🎉 That’s it. Thanks for being a Buddy — it makes a real difference, especially in a remote company where there’s no office hallway to casually bump into people. The new Toggler’s first impression of our culture was partly shaped by you.

If things feel off

Sometimes onboarding doesn’t go smoothly. Here’s what to do:

  • If they seem disengaged or struggling → mention it to their Lead in a private DM. Keep it factual: ‘I noticed X’ not ‘I think they’re failing.’
  • If they share something personal or sensitive → listen, be empathetic, and suggest they talk to People Ops if it’s affecting their work.
  • If YOU are struggling with the Buddy role → that’s okay too. Talk to People Ops. We can adjust.

You don’t need to fix anything. You just need to notice and communicate.
