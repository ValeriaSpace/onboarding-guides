👋 Welcome to Toggl! You're joining a fully remote, bootstrapped company of ~100 people spread across 40+ countries. We care about focus, ownership, and doing great work without unnecessary noise. This guide is your map for the next 8 weeks — what to learn, who to talk to, and how to find your rhythm here.

How this guide works

This is a living document, not a policy manual. Here's how to read it:

  • Checkboxes are tasks — tick them off as you go. Some are time-sensitive, most are flexible.
  • Page links take you to deeper resources in our Notion workspace. You don't need to read everything on day one.
  • Each week has a theme. The early weeks are structured; the later weeks give you more room to find your own pace.
  • Your Lead has a separate Command Center tracking your onboarding from their side. Your Buddy has a guide too. You're not doing this alone.

If something feels unclear, ask. Seriously. We default to transparency here, and 'I don't understand this yet' is always a valid thing to say.

Things worth knowing

What if I can't finish everything in a given week?

That's fine. These are guideposts, not deadlines. If you're consistently falling behind, talk to your Lead — it might mean the pace needs adjusting, or you need something unblocked.

How async are we, really?

Very. Default to writing things down. Post in channels, not DMs. Don't expect instant replies. If something is truly urgent (rare), say so explicitly. Your Buddy can help you figure out the communication norms for your team.

When should I start tracking my time?

Day 1. We build time tracking tools — using them is part of how we work. It also helps you understand where your time goes during onboarding.

Is it okay to push back on how things are done?

Yes. We value directness. If you see something that doesn't make sense, say so. The Fresh Eyes Report exists for exactly this reason, but don't wait until Week 7 — bring it up whenever.

What if I feel overwhelmed?

Normal. You're learning a new company, new people, new tools, and new ways of working — all at once and all remotely. Most people say the first month is the hardest. Talk to your Buddy, your Lead, or People Ops. Nobody expects you to have it all figured out yet.

What if something feels off but I can't quite name it?

That's worth mentioning too. Your Lead and People Ops would rather hear 'something feels weird but I'm not sure what' than find out three months later. Early and vague is better than late and specific.

Professional

  • Fresh Eyes Audit details

    🧐 The Fresh Eyes Audit

    You have a superpower right now that you will lose in exactly 3 weeks: Ignorance. You see things we don't — where our documentation is confusing, where our processes feel heavy, where something just doesn't make sense. Your mission: Before you lose your fresh eyes, audit us. Be ruthless.

    The 3 Questions

    1. The "WTF" Moment: What was the one process or tool setup that made you want to scream? Don't propose a fix yet — just name the pain.
    2. The Ghost Town: Which Notion page or Slack channel seems dead, outdated, or misleading?
    3. The "Why do we do it this way?": Identify one ritual — a meeting, a report, a workflow — that feels like it adds no value.

    How to submit: Share it directly with TA. We promise to address at least one thing you identify.
